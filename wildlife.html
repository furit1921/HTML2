Parastratiosphecomyia stratiosphecomyioides (Southeast Asian soldier fly)

Parastratiosphecomyia stratiosphecomyioides is a species of fly in the family Stratiomyidae. It is known for having one of the longest scientific names of any animal species. This fly is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive appearance, which includes a wasp-like body and striking coloration. The name "Parastratiosphecomyia stratiosphecomyioides" reflects its resemblance to wasps, with "stratiosphecomyioides" meaning "resembling Stratiosphecomyia."

Sacabambaspis

Sacabambaspis is an extinct genus of armored jawless fish which lived in the Ordovician period. Sacabambaspis inhabited shallow waters on the continental margins of the southern supercontinent Gondwana

panda (but goofy ahh animal)

The giant panda, also known as the panda bear or simply panda, is a bear species endemic to China . It is characterised by its white coat with black patches around the eyes, ears, legs and shoulders. Its body is rotund; adult individuals weigh 100 to 115 kg and are typically 1.2 to 1.9 m long.

african wild dog (Lycaon pictus)

The African wild dog (Lycaon pictus), also called painted dog and Cape hunting dog, is a wild canine native to sub-Saharan Africa . It is the largest wild canine in Africa, and the only extant member of the genus Lycaon, which is distinguished from Canis by dentition highly specialised for a hypercarnivorous diet and by a lack of dewclaws.
